Early Life

I was born in Kocani, Macedonia, on a really hot summer day, 26.07.2001. I know mum and dad bought a TV from the money people gave for me, the baby. Why do people even give money to babies?

Anyway, I know I had been in safe hands, I was mum and dad's first-born, so naturally, they wanted everything to be perfect with me.

Then, I grew up a little. This part of my life I remember spending half of the time with my parents, and half of the time with my grandma in Radovis. This went on for quite some time, and I absolutely loved it, both places.

Even at the age of just 10, I had a passion for computers. Well... mostly gaming at the time, and computers were a little bit a rare thing to have at the time. I remember playing the Nintendo until I fall asleep with the controller in my hands.

School And Me

I've always got praises, from every school I've went to. I was taught from an early age that school is the most important thing. Growing up, I've come to realize that's not quite true. And, there are a lot of aspects that need to be looked at when talking about this. God, I could talk for hours about this, but I won't go into any more details here.

What separates me, what has always separated me, from the average person, is my curious side, my experimental, thought-provoking side. I absolutely want to understand everything, from 0 to the point it is at now, It's what gives me joy, what keeps me forward.

It's probably the reason why I've done so well in school too. People always tell me I am quite clever, and I probably am, even though I've learned to deny that as a way of being modest.

My Passion For Coding

Okay, so from what I've told you so far, you can guess, why does coding light up sparks in me.

But here it is in my words. From the moment I got introduced to coding, around 2012, I fell in love with it. For me, it was crazy how, you start with a blank nothingness, you find a problem, any problem, and you code a solution to it, and it's yours... It's your contribution to the world, and words can't describe what that acknowledgement did for me.

Fast forward couple of years of learning, I now have a couple of fun projects, with the most notable being an online version of the Uno game, and a Creativity Corner for listening to music. More about these in the projects page.
